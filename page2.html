Graeme Fox

PhD (Manchester Metropolitan University)
MSc, BSc (Hons) (The University of Liverpool)


I am currently a Post-Doctoral Research Associate at Manchester Metropolitan University, where I am interested in using genomics approaches to analyse the diet and gut microbiome of bees. I have a very general interest in ecological genetics and particularly in next-generation sequencing technologies and bioinformatics, and the exciting new ways they can be used to answer biological questions.
Previously, I have worked as a Research Associate at Manchester Metropolitan University researching next-generation sequencing methods and applications in conservation and ecology, and a Research Technician at The University of Manchester in both the soil ecology group and genomics core facility.

Work In The Press

Some recent work the department performed on trade in 'hatha jodi': a root believed to bring good luck in some cultures. Trade in the plant has been fuelling illicit trade in animal parts.
This work has been featured at Gizmodo, The Telegraph, and the BBC amongst others, with a range of great headlines.

My work on captive populations of Raja undulata has been featured on BBC Earth and Manchester University News.
